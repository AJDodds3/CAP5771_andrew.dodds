What is the central question? Can stock news authors be classified based on the sentiment they express and subsequent market behavior.

Why is it important in context? Our markets are far from free and fair and one of the mechanisms used to manipulate retail sentiment is news sites and individual authors pushing out skewed information which hurts everyday people who do not have all the time in the world to research. Having a reliable mechanism to classify authors can be a beneficial indicator of reliability and trust for valid information.

What would "success" look like? Author's classification remains the same after prediction based on new articles they write. The goal is not to predict the stock market or stock performance but to evaluate the authors ability to properly frame information in way that reflects what is actually happening in a company.

What variables are essential, and why? Author name, age, experience, whether they work independently or for a company, stock they are writing about, article publish data, stock performance on multiple time intervals after the article, article sentiment

Who will use the results? Anyone that is reading about the markets will use the authors writing score to determine how much to trust the article

Is this realistic given constraints? Yes but not for every author, every article, and every stock for a semester project
