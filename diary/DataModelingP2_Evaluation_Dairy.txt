This file is an overview of the final steps of modeling

I am excited to talk to you in class about the results I got for this week because I think they are actually interesting. I had never
actually done an unsupervised learning task before this so it was interesting to learn. I used KMeans clustering to group the publishers as 
a way to understand what kind of publisher they are given my raw data. The standard required steps for this week did not apply
for unsupervised learning like train test split, the standard evaluation metrics, and confusion matrices. Instead I used a silhouette
score for my 2 models which is basically a measure of how well they are grouping. My models received 0.18 and 0.187 which is actually 
not that bad for financial data. My second model had 3 clusters and the first group had strong opinions and almost completely flat performance
meaning your return following them will be about 0-1%. The second group had extreme negative opinions but tended to be wrong a lot which is really interesting,
and the last group were the very neutral authors that just state facts and do not offer much meaningful opinion or analysis.
